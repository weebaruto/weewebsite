## 👤 Persona Overview

**Name:** Bobby<br  />
**Role:** Lifelong music collector, seasoned technologist, occasional intern<br  />
**Location:** Northern Ireland / Japan (frequently between the two)<br  />
**Experience:** 30+ years in tech; 40+ years in music<br  />
**Background:** Started buying records at ten with rooftop money. Never stopped. Spent three decades doing the work the way the work gets done — and recently took ninety days to find out what was left when he wasn't.<br  />
**Work Environment:** Periods of intense, heads-down work punctuated by deliberate resets<br  />
**Traits:** Reflective, direct, culturally curious, values authenticity over performance, drawn to the understated<br  />

---

## 🎯 Motivations

- Hold on to the reflection ritual that kept him grounded through 90-in-90
- Find a moment of quiet in days that move too fast
- Build something personal that travels with him — not a notebook, not a streaming queue
- Grow a collection that reflects who he is, not what an algorithm thinks he wants
- Leave something worth passing on to the people who matter

---

## 🏆 Goals

- Arrive at a resonant lyric without having to search for one
- Read something in the morning that sets a tone worth carrying into the day
- Have a single place where the words that matter actually live
- Add to the collection without it feeling like a task
- Share a lyric with someone who needs it — simply, without ceremony

---

## 😣 Frustrations

- The bull-clip book doesn't travel and doesn't grow
- Streaming platforms optimise for noise, not quiet — the algorithm doesn't know what he needs today
- He has to remember to seek out the thing that grounds him; the ritual gets crowded out
- Good lyrics are scattered across apps, memory, notebooks, and half-opened tabs
- He starts to add a song to the collection and then doesn't — the friction wins
- Nothing serves the moment between two thoughts when you need to land somewhere

---

## 📈 Measures of Success

- Opens it daily without being prompted or reminded
- At least one lyric per session that actually lands — he feels it, not just reads it
- The collection grows week over week without it feeling like effort
- The reflection ritual survives beyond the ninety days that started it
- He shares something with someone and it says what he couldn't

---

> **Summary:**
> Bobby is not looking for a music app. He already has those. He is looking for a quiet corner of the internet that hands him the right words on any given day — words chosen by him, not for him. A Song For Life is the digital version of that dog-eared bull-clip book: personal, unhurried, and always worth opening.
